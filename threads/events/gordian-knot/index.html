Gordian Knot

"Mathematics is not a careful march down a well-cleared highway, but a journey into a strange wilderness, where the explorers often get lost. Rigour should be a signal to the historian that the maps have been made, and the real explorers have gone elsewhere." — W.S. Anglin

Gordian Knot is a bunch of extremely tangled knots from the beautiful world of mathematics. It presents to you an opportunity to sneak into the grand architecture and design of the universe, which belongs to numbers and figures. Aided by any computational tool at your behest, can you explore the beauty of mathematics, and reach at solutions to problems that ask for insights which can only come if one chooses to get lost in the journey of mathematics?

The event has ended.

Rules

  1. It is a contest based on problems of mathematics and computing, inspired by Project Euler. All problems will require some mathematical ingenuity to reach at a solution or a decent method that can solve the problem quickly.
  2. The problems can come from a wide range of topics, especially number theory, geometry and algebra.
  3. For every problem, only the solution, as a string, must be submitted.
