Kernel Cruise

“The major cause [of the software crisis] is that the machines have become several orders of magnitude more powerful! To put it quite bluntly: as long as there were no machines, programming was no problem at all; when we had a few weak computers, programming became a mild problem, and now we have gigantic computers, programming has become an equally gigantic problem.” - Edsger Dijkstra

Felicity Threads 2k14 presents to you Kernel Cruise, a platform where you’ll be pitched against various computational problems, varying from combinatorial to numerical in nature, on a General Purpose GPU compuing environment.
GPUs are data parallel machines, with a multitude of processors on single chip capable of performing similar computations over chunks of a large amount of data, and involves vector processing. They are programmed using frameworks such CUDA, which is available for Nvidia GPU machines.
Programming parallel machines involves much greater skill than programming single core machines, as vectorization, synchronization, and distribution come up as major challenges. And it is this dragon of parallelism, that we invite you to battle.
And if you haven't faced this dragon before, don't worry, as we'll be having familiarization phase for one week before the contest, wherein you'll be given access to machines, in addition to problems and tutorials, for learning and practice.

Rules

  2. The contest will be held on February 9th, 2014 and will be preceded by a week long practice and familiarization phase.
